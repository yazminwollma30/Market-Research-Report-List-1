<p><ul><li>Fat Turpentine</li><li>Sulphate Turpentine</li><li>Wood Turpentine</li><li>Carbonization Turpentine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The turpentine oil market comprises various types, each derived through distinct processes. **Fat Turpentine** is obtained from the distillation of pine resin, commonly used in paints and solvents. **Sulphate Turpentine** is a byproduct of the kraft pulping process in paper manufacturing, often utilized in fragrances and chemicals. **Wood Turpentine** is extracted from pine wood chips, primarily used in industrial applications. **Carbonization Turpentine** is produced during wood carbonization, finding niche uses in specialty chemicals. Each type caters to specific industries, reflecting its unique extraction method and application.</p></p>
