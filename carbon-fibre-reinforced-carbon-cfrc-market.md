<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) is a composite material known for its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and thermal resistance, making it ideal for aerospace and automotive applications. The CFRC market is primarily driven by two manufacturing processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP). CVD involves depositing carbon onto a substrate, creating a strong, uniform material. LIP, on the other hand, involves impregnating a carbon matrix with liquid precursors, enhancing structural integrity and performance in high-temperature environments.</p></p>
