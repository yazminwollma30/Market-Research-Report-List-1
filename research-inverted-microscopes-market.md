<p><p>Research inverted microscopes are categorized into monocular, binocular, and trinocular types based on their optical configurations. Monocular microscopes feature a single eyepiece, offering a straightforward view suitable for basic observations. Binocular models come with two eyepieces, providing enhanced depth perception and comfort for extended viewing sessions. Trinocular microscopes include an additional third eyepiece, allowing simultaneous viewing and image capturing, making them ideal for documentation and advanced research applications. Each type caters to specific research needs and user preferences.</p></p>
