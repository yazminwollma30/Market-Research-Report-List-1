<p><p>IC cards and smart cards are categorized into two main market types: contact and contactless. Contact cards require physical insertion into a reader, enabling secure data transfer through direct electrical connection. They are commonly used for banking and identification. In contrast, contactless cards utilize radio-frequency identification (RFID) technology, allowing transactions to be completed without direct contact. This makes them ideal for applications like public transport and mobile payments, enhancing user convenience and speed in daily transactions.</p></p>
